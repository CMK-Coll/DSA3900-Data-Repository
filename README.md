# Thesis: Time Series Analysis of Air Pollutant Emissions and Electrical Access in Kenya

---

## Introduction

This study is meant to address the question: **"Is electrification better or worse for us?"**

Kenya has been steadily increasing its electrification status, with more rural areas now having access to power that they otherwise did not have, increasing to approximately 80% as of now. Therefore, an important question arises: is this progress for **us**?

“**Us**” in the context of this study refers to the long-term collective view of our environment, a factor that impacts both the wildlife around us and ourselves.

Therefore, the project aims to analyse the impact electrification has on the environment and the factors that can cause its variation. Using the information we have now, can we then predict future trends?

---

## How the Analysis was carried out

The analysis collects data and correlates their significance against each other, determining whether the increase in electrification has caused a general rise or fall in CO₂ pollution levels, from which conclusions can be drawn.

It attempts to correlate and predict trends based on observed data using regression models to understand general trends and patterns, and transformer models to predict future patterns.
